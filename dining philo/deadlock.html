Deadlock conditions

Understanding circular wait and system deadlock

Demonstration of the four Coffman conditions in action: when every philosopher acquires one fork and waits for another, the system enters deadlock.

  • Mutual exclusion Only one philosopher can hold a fork at any moment.
  • Hold & wait Each philosopher clutches one fork while waiting for the next.
  • No preemption Forks cannot be forcibly reclaimed; releases must be voluntary.
  • Circular wait Everyone waits for the fork to their right, forming a closed loop.

Why it matters

Deadlock is costly in modern systems.

Threads stuck in circular waits hog CPU time, devices, and locks. Operating systems must detect, avoid, or prevent these conditions to keep applications responsive.

Resource hogging

Stalled threads still own resources, starving others that could make progress.

Detection overhead

Cycle detection in wait-for graphs consumes time; prevention is often cheaper.

User impact

From frozen UIs to stalled servers, deadlocks manifest as critical system reliability and performance issues.
